<p><p>Scattering-based optical sensors leverage various scattering phenomena to analyze materials. Raman sensors utilize inelastic light scattering to identify molecular compositions through vibrational energy levels. Rayleigh sensors rely on elastic scattering to measure changes in light scattering caused by particle size or concentration in a medium. Brillouin sensors exploit scattering from density fluctuations, providing information on temperature and strain in materials. Together, these technologies enable precise monitoring and characterization in applications ranging from environmental analysis to structural health monitoring.</p></p>
