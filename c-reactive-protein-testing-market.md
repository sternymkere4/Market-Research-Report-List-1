<p><p>C-Reactive Protein (CRP) testing market encompasses various testing methodologies. ELISA (Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay) is widely used for its sensitivity and specificity in measuring CRP levels. Immunoturbidimetric assays offer rapid results through light scattering technology. Chemiluminescence Immunoassay (CLIA) provides enhanced sensitivity with lower sample volumes. High-sensitivity C-Reactive Protein (hsCRP) assays are crucial for early cardiovascular disease detection, measuring lower concentrations of CRP. Together, these methods facilitate effective diagnosis and monitoring of inflammatory conditions and cardiovascular risks.</p></p>
